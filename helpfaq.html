How to Use DraftDeck Fantasy and Play Fantasy Football
 Welcome to DraftDeck! This guide will help you understand how to use our platform and enjoy the exciting world of fantasy football. If you have further questions, feel free to contact us through the Support tab.

Help & FAQ

Getting Started
1. Sign Up:
Create a free account with your email or connect via Google.
2. Join a League:
Click "Join a League" to find a public league or enter a private league code sent by your friends.
Alternatively, create your own league and invite others to join!
3. Draft Your Team:
Participate in the live draft to select players for your team.
Use player profiles styled as trading cards to compare stats and insights.

FAQs
Q: Can I play if I’m new to fantasy football? A: Absolutely! DraftDeck is designed for beginners and seasoned players alike. Our trading card-style player profiles make it easy to understand stats and performance.
Q: How do I find free agents or make trades? A: Navigate to the Player Market tab to view free agents or propose trades to other players. Use filters to search by position, performance, or availability.
Q: What happens if a player on my team gets injured? A: Injured players can be moved to the Injured Reserve (IR) spot on your roster. Replace them by picking up a free agent or making a trade.
Q: How do scoring rules work? A: Scoring varies by league, but typical points are awarded for actions like touchdowns, yards gained, and receptions. Check your league’s specific rules under the Scoring Settings tab.

How to Play
1. Set Your Lineup:
Choose players to start each week. Drag and drop trading cards to fill positions like quarterback, running back, and wide receiver.
Keep an eye on player injuries or bye weeks!
2. Score Points:
Your players earn points based on their real-life performance in games (e.g., touchdowns, rushing yards).
Monitor live scores through our Game Tracker.
3. Make Trades and Pickups:
Trade players with others in your league or add free agents to improve your roster.
4. Compete Weekly:
Face off against other league members. The team with the most points wins the matchup.
5. Win the League:
At the end of the season, the team with the best record or highest playoff performance is crowned champion.
